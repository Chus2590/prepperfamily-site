📚 Research Sources

One click to copy all the key sources for this guide so you can research further:

1. USDA Complete Guide to Home Canning, 2020 ed. (Chapters 1 & 3) — Official guide from the USDA; sets safety standards (pressure, times, altitudes) for meats and other low-acid foods.
2. National Center for Home Food Preservation – "Meat, Strips, Cubes or Chunks" — Detailed factsheet by the University of Georgia with step-by-step instructions for red meats, following USDA guidelines.
3. So Easy to Preserve, 6th ed. (Univ. of Georgia Cooperative Extension, 2021) — Reference with scientific foundations and verified procedures for home preservation; pressure canning chapter for meats and poultry.
4. Ball Blue Book Guide to Preserving, 37th ed. (2024) — Practical manual from the leading jar brand; includes time tables and operational tips that complement official standards.
5. Penn State Extension – "Home Food Preservation: Pressure Canning" — Academic publication explaining the science behind pressure canning and the need for >240 °F for meats.
